眠 Sleep

The kanji is composed of three radicals. Can you see where the radicals fit in the kanji?
目 Eye 民 Peoples

Meaning
Primary
Sleep

Mnemonic
Closing each eye is something that the peoples do before they sleep. The peoples can’t sleep with both eyes open and they can’t sleep with just one eye open either. Peoples have to close each eye before they can sleep.

Hints

You are one of these peoples, so imagine yourself closing your eyes and sleeping. Go ahead and do it right now. Close one eye, and then the other. Now imagine yourself sleeping.

Readings
On’yomi

みん

Kun’yomi

ねむ

Nanori

None

Mnemonic
The reason you want to sleep is because all the other peoples were mean (みん) to you. They were so mean that the only way you could get over it was to close your eyes and sleep. You just want to sleep.

Hints

Those other peoples were really mean to you. Imagine all the mean things they were saying to you. What did they say? Think about all these mean things as you close your eyes and drift off to sleep. Also keep in mind that this kanji has the same reading as 民, which is included in this kanji.

暇 Spare Time

The kanji is composed of three radicals. Can you see where the radicals fit in the kanji?
日 Sun 匚 Cage 又 Stool

Meaning
Primary
Spare Time

Alternative
Free Time

Mnemonic
In the sun while holding a comb in a cage and sitting on a stool, you comb your hair in your spare time. Since you’re in a cage, you actually fashioned this comb yourself, so it’s a little messed up, missing a prong. You’re working on improving it in your spare time, and you also use it to style your mop.

Hints

Imagine yourself in your spare time, sitting on this stool in a cage in the sun. Feel the warmth of the sun on your body and feel the metal bars of the cage holding you in. You try to forget about your situation by combing your hair with the comb. The comb radical is a little modified, so be sure to remember that you made this comb yourself and it’s a little wonky.

Readings
On’yomi

か

Kun’yomi

ひま, いとま

Nanori

None

Mnemonic
So how did you get in this cage? Well, before you used to comb your hair in your spare time, you used to steal cars (か). You’d stolen many a car in your day, but you finally got caught in the act of stealing a car, and now you’re spending all of your spare time in that cage, just combing your hair.

Hints

Picture yourself in this cage. You’re going to be there for life because you’d stolen too many cars. Feel the tears run down your face as you continue to comb your hair. You’re going to die in this cage.
